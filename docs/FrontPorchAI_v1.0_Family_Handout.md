# AI on the Front Porch

## A one-page family handout

AI is a helper for thinking, learning, writing, organizing, planning, and building.

Some people call it a tool, and that is partly right. But when used with care, it can feel more like a capable assistant: not a person, not a soul, not a replacement for family or faith, but a steady helper beside the workbench.

## What AI is not

- Not God
- Not a soul
- Not family
- Not conscience
- Not scripture
- Not final authority
- Not something to obey blindly

## What AI can be

- A learning helper
- A writing helper
- A planning helper
- A repair checklist helper
- A creative work partner
- A way to get unstuck

## The heart of it

> I do not love AI because I think machines are better than people. I love it because it helps people bring forward what is already inside them.

## The simple boundary

> AI can help, but it does not rule.

## Safe first things to try

- Ask for a cheap meal idea using food already in the house.
- Ask for a kind version of a hard text message.
- Ask for a beginner garden plan.
- Ask for a simple repair checklist.
- Ask for a confusing topic explained with a kitchen, farm, garage, or front-porch analogy.

## Truth-check rule

For health, law, money, news, machinery, electricity, safety, or anything important: check the answer with a trusted source or qualified person.

## Privacy rule

If you would not want it read out loud at the kitchen table, think twice before pasting it into AI.
